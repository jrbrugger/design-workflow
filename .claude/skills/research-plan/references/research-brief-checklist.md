# Research Brief Checklist

Use this checklist to validate your research brief before sharing with stakeholders. Every item should be checked before the brief leaves your hands.

---

## Project Scope
- [ ] Problem is stated clearly in 1–2 sentences
- [ ] "Why now" trigger is explicit (not just "we want to know more")
- [ ] Scope boundaries are defined — what's IN and what's OUT
- [ ] A stakeholder or sponsor is named
- [ ] Problem is framed neutrally (not leading toward a specific solution)

## Research Goals
- [ ] 2–4 goals are listed (not more, not fewer)
- [ ] Each goal starts with an action verb (Understand, Identify, Evaluate, Discover)
- [ ] No goal uses "Validate" or "Prove" (research reveals, it doesn't confirm)
- [ ] Each goal is specific enough to know when it's achieved
- [ ] Goals are about learning, not about deliverables
- [ ] Every goal connects to a decision that needs to be made

## Target Group
- [ ] Primary user segment is defined
- [ ] Behavioral criteria are included (not just demographics)
- [ ] Recruitment criteria are specific enough to screen against
- [ ] Sample size is stated with reasoning
- [ ] Exclusion criteria are noted
- [ ] Secondary segment defined (if relevant)

## Known Insights
- [ ] Facts and assumptions are clearly separated
- [ ] Every fact has a source and date
- [ ] Every assumption has a confidence level
- [ ] You've checked analytics, support tickets, and past research
- [ ] Team assumptions are documented (not just your own)
- [ ] This section is not empty — you always know SOMETHING

## Main Research Question
- [ ] It's ONE sentence
- [ ] Starts with "How", "What", "Why", or "In what ways"
- [ ] It's neutral (not leading)
- [ ] Answerable through research (not a business decision)
- [ ] Broad enough to encompass all sub-questions
- [ ] Specific enough to be actionable

## Sub-Questions
- [ ] 3–6 sub-questions listed
- [ ] Each maps to at least one research goal
- [ ] Each has a tagged research method
- [ ] Mix of "what", "how", and "why" questions
- [ ] Ordered from broad context → specific details
- [ ] Collectively they answer the main research question
- [ ] No duplicate or overlapping questions

## Hypotheses
- [ ] 2–4 hypotheses listed
- [ ] Each is falsifiable (research can prove them wrong)
- [ ] Each follows the format: "We believe X because Y"
- [ ] Each includes success criteria: "We'll know this when Z"
- [ ] Based on assumptions from Section 4
- [ ] You're genuinely willing to be wrong about each one

## Research Methods
- [ ] Method(s) match the question types
- [ ] Justification given for why this method (not just what)
- [ ] Participant count is stated per method
- [ ] Format specified (remote/in-person, moderated/unmoderated)
- [ ] Duration per session is estimated
- [ ] Research type identified (Generative/Descriptive/Evaluative/Causal)
- [ ] Methods are feasible within the timeline

## Test Metrics
- [ ] Quantitative metrics have specific thresholds (numbers, percentages)
- [ ] Baselines are included where available
- [ ] Qualitative indicators are defined
- [ ] "Good enough" is clearly defined
- [ ] Metrics connect to research goals
- [ ] No vague measures ("users like it", "high satisfaction")

## Business Impact
- [ ] Connected to a specific business metric (revenue, retention, NPS, etc.)
- [ ] Impact category stated (Quality, Cost, or Sales)
- [ ] Specific decisions this research will inform are listed
- [ ] Named stakeholders who will act on findings
- [ ] Clear explanation of "so what" — why the business should care

## Planning & Timeline
- [ ] Week-by-week breakdown provided
- [ ] Each week has activities AND deliverables
- [ ] Dependencies are listed (recruitment, reviews, tool access)
- [ ] Buffer time included for recruitment delays
- [ ] Key milestones marked
- [ ] Final share-out date set
- [ ] Timeline is realistic (not compressed beyond reason)

---

## Red Flags — Fix Before Sharing

These indicate structural problems with the brief:

- **No "why now"** — If you can't explain why this research matters right now, stakeholders won't prioritize it
- **Goals that are really tasks** — "Conduct 6 interviews" is an activity, not a goal
- **Empty Known Insights** — You always know something. An empty section means you haven't done your homework
- **Leading research question** — "Why is our checkout bad?" assumes it is. Rephrase neutrally
- **No metrics thresholds** — "Improve satisfaction" means nothing without a number
- **Methods without justification** — "We'll do interviews" isn't enough. WHY interviews?
- **Missing business impact** — Research without business connection gets deprioritized
- **Timeline without dependencies** — Recruitment delays are the #1 timeline killer

---

## Cross-Check: Does Everything Connect?

Run this final check across sections:

| Check | Sections | Question |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Goals → Questions | 2 → 5, 6 | Does every goal have sub-questions that address it? |
| Questions → Methods | 6 → 8 | Does every sub-question have a method to answer it? |
| Assumptions → Hypotheses | 4 → 7 | Are your assumptions turned into testable hypotheses? |
| Methods → Timeline | 8 → 11 | Does the timeline allocate enough time for each method? |
| Goals → Metrics | 2 → 9 | Does every goal have a way to measure success? |
| Metrics → Impact | 9 → 10 | Do your metrics connect to business outcomes? |
| Scope → Goals | 1 → 2 | Do goals stay within the defined scope? |

**If any row fails, revise the brief before sharing.**
